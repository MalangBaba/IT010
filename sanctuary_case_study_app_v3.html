Sanctuary ITIL® Transformation Journey

A Real-World Case Study in IT Service Management

Executive Summary

Sanctuary, a UK-based social housing and care provider managing over 120,000 homes across England and Scotland, embarked on a comprehensive ITIL® 4 transformation journey. This case study demonstrates how a large not-for-profit organization identified critical gaps in IT Service Management maturity and systematically implemented best practices to improve incident management, problem management, configuration management, and cross-team collaboration.

warning The Challenge: Before ITIL®

school 1. Knowledge Gaps

Knowledge scattered across teams with no centralized repository. "Wheel re-invention" was common as solutions weren't shared.

local_fire_department 2. Reactive Firefighting

Recurring incidents were treated as new problems each time. No systematic root-cause analysis meant the same fires burned daily.

emergency 3. Inconsistent Response

Response times varied. Unclear escalation paths led to communication breakdowns during critical major incidents.

hub 4. Configuration Chaos

Lack of standardized asset tracking. Unclear which IT components belonged to which services, making change risky.

check_circle The Solution

1

Phase 1: Awareness & Culture (Weeks 1-4)

  • ITIL® 4 Awareness Program: 100% attendance across operational teams.
  • Foundation Certification: 34 practice owners certified.
  • Apollo 13 Simulation: 8 full runs to apply concepts in a safe environment.
2

Phase 2: Structural Implementation (Weeks 5-12)

  • Knowledge Mgmt: Centralized repository created.
  • Problem Mgmt: 5 Whys & Known Errors database established.
  • Major Incident: Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and clear escalation paths (L1→L2→L3).
  • Configuration: CMDB initiative launched to map assets.
