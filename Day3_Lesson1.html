Change Management

Controlling the lifecycle of all changes to minimize disruption.

The Traffic Control of IT

Change Management (or Change Enablement) ensures that changes are recorded, evaluated, authorised, prioritised, planned, tested, implemented, documented, and reviewed in a controlled manner. It is not about stopping change, but making change safer.

Types of Change

1. Standard Change: Pre-authorised, low risk, follows a procedure (e.g., password reset). 2. Normal Change: Needs assessment and authorisation (e.g., upgrading a server). 3. Emergency Change: Urgent, must be done immediately to restore service (e.g., security patch during an attack).

The CAB

The Change Advisory Board (CAB) supports the Change Manager in assessing significant changes. They review risks, business impact, and readiness.
